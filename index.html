Subject: Individuals and Societies - Integrated Humanities

Grade: MYP Year 1 | Duration: 15 hours

Key Concept: Global Interactions | Related Concepts: Culture, Identity

Global Context: Identities and Relationships

Statement of Inquiry: Global interactions shape the formation of identities, cultural roles, and the status of individuals within different types of societies.

Unit Overview: This comprehensive unit planner explores how students understand different types of societies (hunter-gatherer, pastoral, agricultural, industrial, post-industrial) and how global interactions influence cultural identity, roles, and status. The unit incorporates inquiry-based learning, ATL skills development, and authentic assessments.

Key Learning Experiences:

  • Brainstorming and mind mapping activities on "What makes a society?"
  • Cooperative group research on different types of societies
  • Cultural reflection and gallery walk activities
  • Global interactions simulation through a "world trade fair"
  • Class debate on tradition vs. global change
  • Role model research and presentation

Assessment Structure:

  • Summative Assessment: "Identity under the Global Lens" - A cultural research mini-exhibition where students investigate how global interactions shape identities in a chosen society (Criteria B: Investigating & Criteria C: Communicating)
  • Formative Assessments: Global connections map, gallery walk reflections, mini debates, and role model quick posters

üîÑ IB Continuum Framework Model

Course: IBEC Program | Format: Conceptual Framework Design

Overview: Developed a comprehensive visual framework that integrates the three IB programmes‚Äî Primary Years Programme (PYP), Middle Years Programme (MYP), and Diploma Programme (DP)‚Äîinto one cohesive learning continuum. This assignment demonstrates a deep understanding of how IB education develops progressively across all stages of student learning.

Conceptual Rationale: Rather than viewing PYP, MYP, and DP as separate programmes, this framework presents learning as one continuous journey where each stage builds upon the previous one. The model shows how:

  • PYP develops foundational inquiry skills and curiosity through the inquiry cycle
  • MYP connects ideas to real-life global contexts and interdisciplinary learning
  • DP cultivates deep, independent thinking and academic rigor

Key Framework Components:

  • IB Learner Profile at the center - emphasizing attributes like thinkers, communicators, caring, and open-minded across all programmes
  • Approaches to Learning (ATL) Skills - showing progression of thinking, research, communication, social, and self-management skills
  • Global Contexts - demonstrating how students connect classroom learning to real-world issues at every stage
  • Inquiry-Based Approach - highlighting how questioning, exploration, and reflection develop throughout the continuum

Educational Significance: This framework illustrates how IB education creates meaningful, balanced, and connected learning experiences that help students develop as global citizens. It emphasizes that learning is not compartmentalized but rather a continuous journey where skills and understanding deepen progressively, always connected to students' identity and role in the world.

Skills Demonstrated: Conceptual thinking, visual framework design, understanding of IB philosophy and pedagogy, ability to synthesize complex educational structures, and communication of interconnected learning progressions.
